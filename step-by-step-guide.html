Skin Tone Profile

Know your colours before you choose them

Begin Profile →
  • Identify your skin type — Fair, Medium-Olive, Tan, or Dark-Deep — and confirm your undertone through three simple at-home tests: vein colour, jewellery preference, and fabric response
  • Receive a curated colour palette of Best and Highly Acceptable shades and combinations calibrated precisely to your unique skin profile and undertone

  • Navigate our interactive 3D body guide — hover each measurement point to illuminate the exact placement on the model and take your own measurements precisely at home
  • Record 18+ body measurements including height, shoulder width, chest, waist, full hip, arm length, inseam, and more — in centimetres or inches — with built-in proportion validation
  • Saved to your account and auto-applied at every checkout — we strongly advise refreshing your measurements before any commission placed 45 days or more apart. The body is not static, and neither should your fit be

III

Fabric Fundamentals

Cloth is the canvas — choose it wisely

Explore Fabric Fundamentals →
  • Master the language of cloth — thread count in cotton, lea count in linen, and how fibre density governs handle, weight, and the way a garment moves and drapes on the body
  • Understand the art of blending: how natural fibres are combined to balance breathability, structure, softness, and long-term durability across seasons and wear
  • A curated selection guide to the finest natural fabrics — cotton, linen, silk, wool, and cashmere — matched by garment type, climate, and occasion to help you choose with confidence
  • Learn to read leather by grade — full-grain retains the natural surface and develops rich patina over time, top-grain offers refined uniformity, corrected-grain delivers consistency; each grade defines how a garment wears and ages
  • Understand the tanning spectrum: vegetable-tanned leathers build depth and character with wear, while chrome-tanned leathers prioritise softness, colour consistency, and resilience under frequent and demanding use
  • A garment-matched leather guide — lambskin for fluid soft-structured jackets, calfskin for formal and tailored wear, suede for casual layering — paired with care principles to preserve each hide across seasons

V

Design Selection

Every commission begins with a silhouette

Go to the Bespoke Boutique →
  • Browse our curated catalogue across four dressing categories — Men, Women, Boy, and Girl — each housing a range of silhouettes purpose-built for bespoke construction, from structured formal wear to fluid layering and occasion pieces
  • Every design serves as the architectural foundation of your commission — the cut, structure, and line are set; the fabric, colour, and finishing detail are entirely yours to define in the steps that follow
  • Use your skin tone palette from Step I and your fabric knowledge from Steps III and IV to inform your choice — design selection is where your personal colour profile and material understanding first meet a physical form
  • Every design carries a skin tone selector — apply your result from Step I to preview precisely how the garment's proposed colours and fabrics harmonise with your skin type and undertone before your commission is confirmed
  • Choose your primary material from a curated range of natural fabrics and leathers suited to the selected garment type, season, and occasion — your fundamentals from Steps III and IV make this decision informed rather than instinctive
  • Commission a two-tone garment: a single piece finished in two complementary fabrics, with a contrasting material applied to the edges, collar, cuffs, or trim — a detail that adds depth, structure, and a signature character unique to the House
  • Your selected design and all available fabric options are fully visible under the Pool of Designs — accessible through the Go to Collection button on your chosen category page within the Bespoke Boutique
